# AI Writing Patterns — Consolidated Detection Database

## 4. Structure — Content Patterns

| # | Pattern | Example | Fix |
|---|---------|---------|-----|
| 1 | Significance inflation | "pivotal moment," "testament to" | State facts plainly |
| 2 | Notability name-dropping | "Featured in Forbes, TechCrunch" | One source with real data |
| 3 | Superficial -ing analyses | "highlighting," "showcasing" | Concrete verbs: "shows," "proves" |
| 4 | Promotional language | "breathtaking," "cutting-edge" | Plain descriptors |
| 5 | Vague attributions | "Experts believe" | Name the expert or cut it |
| 6 | Formulaic structures | "Challenges and Future Prospects" | Write what actually matters |
| 7 | Copula avoidance | "serves as," "stands as" | Just say "is" or "has" |
| 8 | Negative parallelisms | "It is not just X; it is Y" | Pick one framing |
| 9 | Forced rule of three | "innovation, collaboration, excellence" | Only group what belongs together |
| 10 | Synonym cycling | Same concept, different words each time | Say it once, well |
| 11 | False ranges | "from sustainability to innovation" | Name specific things |
| 12 | Passive voice / missing actors | "It was determined that..." | Say who determined it |

## 5. Structure — Opening Phrases to Eliminate

These openers instantly signal AI-generated content:

- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In today's digital age..."
- "In an era of..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Let's delve into..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "As we stand at the crossroads of..."
- "Picture this:"

**Fix:** Start with a fact, question, anecdote, or the point itself.

---

## 6. Structure — Transitional Phrases to Eliminate

- "That being said..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "At its core..."
- "To put it simply..."
- "In essence..."
- "This begs the question..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "Moving forward..."
- "On a broader note..."

**Fix:** Just make the point. If the connection is clear from context, no transition is needed.

---

## 7. Structure — Concluding Phrases to Eliminate

- "In conclusion..."
- "To sum up..."
- "By [doing X], you can [achieve Y]..."
- "In the final analysis..."
- "All things considered..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "The future looks bright..."
- "Only time will tell..."
- "As we move forward..."

**Fix:** End with a specific takeaway, a question, or a call to action. Never end with a platitude.

---

## 8. Structure — Structural Patterns to Eliminate

- "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..." (listing three examples after "whether")
- "It's not just [X], it's also [Y]..."
- "Think of [X] as [elaborate metaphor]..."
- Starting with "By" + gerund: "By understanding X, you can Y..."
- Every section following the same header → bullets → conclusion template
- Every paragraph being exactly 3–4 sentences
- Numbered lists where a paragraph would be more natural

## 9. Style — Punctuation and Formatting Tells

| Pattern | Why It's a Tell | Guideline |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Em dash (—) overuse | AI uses them as a crutch for variety | Max one per page. Use commas, colons, or parentheses instead. |
| Boldface overuse | Every other phrase bolded | Bold only what matters on a skim |
| Inline-header lists | "Key Point: explanation" ×5 | Vary formats or use paragraphs |
| Title Case headings | Every Heading Word Capitalized | Sentence case for headings |
| Decorative emojis | Scattered through professional text | Remove from professional content |
| Bullet-point everything | Even when a paragraph reads better | Use bullets for lists, paragraphs for arguments |

## 10. Communication — Chatbot Artifacts

Remove entirely — these have no place in published content:

- "I hope this helps!"
- "Great question!"
- "That's an excellent point!"
- "As an AI language model..."
- "As of my training data..."
- "I'd be happy to help!"
- "Let me break this down for you..."
- "Here's what you need to know..."
- "Absolutely!"
- "Certainly!"

---

## 11. Filler Words and Empty Intensifiers

These words usually add nothing. Remove them or find specific alternatives:

absolutely, actually, basically, certainly, clearly, definitely, essentially, extremely, fundamentally, incredibly, interestingly, naturally, obviously, quite, really, significantly, simply, surely, truly, ultimately, undoubtedly, very, virtually, remarkably, notably

**Test:** Remove the word. If the sentence means the same thing, cut it.

## 13. Red-Flag Word Quick List

Search-and-destroy these words in any content. Their presence strongly correlates with AI generation:

**Tier 1 (almost always AI):**
delve, tapestry, landscape (metaphorical), nestled, vibrant, pivotal, transformative, holistic, multifaceted, nuanced, robust, comprehensive, seamless

**Tier 2 (frequently AI, sometimes legitimate):**
crucial, moreover, furthermore, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative, leverage, foster, bolster, underscore, streamline, harness, empower, curate, resonate

**Tier 3 (contextual — AI when clustered):**
dynamic, synergy, paradigm, ecosystem, framework, stakeholder, scalable, sustainable, actionable, impactful
